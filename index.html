Research foundation

This isn’t just a study. It’s a response to a real social problem.

Angela’s thesis examines how digital immersion and post-COVID habits have shaped Gen Z relationships, anxiety, and the difficulty of building embodied connection in physical third places, while the study itself responds through scripture, mindfulness, shared burden, and intentional presence.

Project objective

Design a Bible study for coffee shops and everyday third places.

Percolate gives Gen Z professionals a practical path toward spiritual growth, soft skills, and resilient face-to-face relationships through low-pressure embodied interaction, honest discussion, and repeatable rhythms of gathering.

Methodology

Built through research, case studies, and visual analysis.

The thesis combines literature review, study of Gen Z interactive habits, and design analysis around typography, color, hierarchy, and accessibility to shape an experience people would actually use with friends in the real world.

Key finding

Coffee culture can act as a bridge back to real community.

Cafés provide comfort, routine, and low social friction. They make a strong setting for conversation, spiritual support, and resilience against the stress of a digital-first life where comparison, distraction, and pseudo-connection are constant.

How it works

Built for small groups, coffee tables, and honest conversation.

Each week is structured to move from scripture into reflection, discussion, prayer, and embodied practice. It is designed to help people pause, notice what is real, and carry each other with more patience and honesty.

Design logic

The color cycle becomes part of the emotional rhythm.

Angela’s repeating palette gives the study a visual cadence: calm, bright, urgent, grounded, then renewed again. The system becomes part of the experience, helping each week feel distinct while still belonging to one shared journey.

Why it matters

Percolate treats presence as a form of care.

This isn’t content for passive scrolling. It is an invitation to live spiritual life with other people, to share burdens, resist isolation, and practice love in everyday situations.
