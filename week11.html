Log 11 // Wait

Waiting is often part of the recipe, not proof something is wrong.

Week 11 of 12

Week 11

Growing
Together

Coffee Moment

☕

Think about a pour-over coffee. You cannot rush it. If you dump all the water at once, you ruin the extraction. You pour a little, let the grounds bloom, and wait. The waiting is part of the recipe. Ask yourself what you are trying to rush right now.

“But I say, walk by the Spirit, and you will not gratify the desires of the flesh...”
~ Galatians 5:16 (ESV)

Reflection

You are part of a microwave generation living in a slow-cooker world. You want instant maturity, instant clarity, and instant community, but growth has a rhythm that cannot be hacked. Patience is not only waiting. It is the posture you hold while you wait. Sometimes God is letting your life bloom, releasing the pressure and residue of the past so that the final pour can run clear and deep. If you are in a season of delay, do not read it only as a setback. It may be part of the extraction process. Faith, deep friendship, and character all take time to brew.

Discussion Questions

  • Why is waiting so uncomfortable for you? What do you fear you are missing while you wait?
  • What would change if you treated this slow season as part of the recipe instead of an interruption?

Prayer

Ask the Lord to meet you in the waiting, soften your frustration, and teach you how to trust the pace of formation.

Personal Practice

Choose the longest line today and resist the urge to pull out your phone. Use those minutes to breathe and pray for the people around you.

Additional Reading
