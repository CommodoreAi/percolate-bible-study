Log 06 // Show Up

Courage rarely feels cinematic. It usually feels like arriving.

Week 6 of 12

Week 6

Courage to
Show Up

Coffee Moment

☕

Think about the crema on an espresso. It is delicate, but it is also the sign of a well-pulled shot. If you wait too long, it disappears. Community can feel like that too. If you wait for the perfect time to connect, the window closes.

“...Be strong and courageous...for the Lord your God is with you wherever you go.”
~ Joshua 1:9 (ESV)

Reflection

You often imagine courage as something cinematic. Most of the time it is smaller than that. It is the decision to go to the coffee meetup when you would rather stay home and scroll. Anxiety says you are too tired, too awkward, too late, or too out of place. Courage is the spirit of power that tells anxiety it does not get to drive the car. When you show up, you are not only doing something for yourself. You are also becoming a pillar for someone else who almost stayed home too.

Discussion Questions

  • What excuse does your brain give you most often when you avoid gathering?
  • What changes when you treat showing up as obedience instead of performance?

Prayer

Ask the Lord for a right-now kind of courage that moves your body before your anxiety can talk you out of it.

Personal Practice

Commit to one social yes this week that makes you slightly nervous. Do not overthink it. Just show up.

Additional Reading

Brew complete

Showing up is the bravest thing.

Not dramatic courage. Quiet, ordinary, I-will-be-here-again-next-week courage. That is what changes a table into a community.
