Week 7

Strength
in Numbers

Coffee Moment

☕

Look at the elements in front of you: the beans, the water, the heat, the filter, and the cup. Individually, they are just items. Together, they become a ritual. Unity is not sameness. It is different parts working toward the same pour.

“Therefore encourage one another and build one another up, just as you are doing.”
~ 1 Thessalonians 5:11 (ESV)

Reflection

Unity is not uniformity. In a performance-driven world, you are often tempted to hide differences so you can fit in faster. But a healthy body works because it is made of different strengths, different temperaments, and different kinds of grace. Loneliness starts to die when you realize you do not have to be everything. You carry a flavor the person next to you lacks, and they carry a strength that can steady your weakness. When people are tuned to the same frequency of grace, the noise of the world gets quieter.

Discussion Questions

  • Who in your life is the water, and who is the bean?
  • What difference in someone else has become a gift to you instead of a problem?

Prayer

Ask the Lord to make you grateful for the people who bring a different strength to the table, and honest about where you still resist interdependence.

Personal Practice

Find someone in your circle whose flavor is very different from yours. Tell them one way their perspective strengthens the group.
