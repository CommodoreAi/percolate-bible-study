Log 10 // Presence

Attention is one of the clearest forms of care.

Week 10 of 12

Week 10

Practicing
Presence

Coffee Moment

☕

Look around the room. Notice the hum of the espresso machine, the murmur of voices, and the weight of the phone in your pocket. Then turn the phone face down. As you take a sip, stay fully with the taste: acidity, sweetness, warmth. For these few minutes, be nowhere else but here.

“Be still, and know that I am God...”
~ Psalm 46:10 (ESV)

Reflection

You are living in a state of continuous partial attention. Physically, you are at the table. Mentally, you are in an inbox, a feed, or the next task. That divided presence is one reason loneliness can survive even in a crowded room. Real presence is a gift you give to another person. It says, “You matter more than the interruption.” When you practice presence with God and with people, the noise of anxiety starts to fade. To be fully available across the table, you often have to choose to be unavailable to the digital world.

Discussion Questions

  • When was the last time someone gave you their undivided attention? How did it make you feel?
  • What usually pulls your mind away when your body is still in the room?

Prayer

Ask the Lord to quiet the parts of you that are always reaching elsewhere and to make your attention feel like a shelter for the people in front of you.

Personal Practice

For your next three meals or coffee dates, keep your phone out of sight entirely. Notice how the conversation changes.
