Week 5

Suffering
& Anxiety

Coffee Moment

☕

Think about the coffee bean. Before it reaches your cup, it is dried, peeled, and roasted in extreme heat. Without the fire, the flavor stays locked inside. The heat does not destroy the bean; it awakens its purpose.

“For God gave us a spirit not of fear but of power and love and self-control.”
~ 2 Timothy 1:7 (ESV)

Reflection

Nobody signs up for suffering. Most of the time you try to optimize your way around pain, pressure, and anxiety. But what if a season of heat is not proof that you are failing, but evidence that something deeper is being formed in you? Hardship strips away what is superficial and forces you to look for a foundation that will actually hold. Growth rarely happens in easy moments. It happens when you stay at the table even when the conversation gets heavy. In community, you do not just share the highlights. You share the heat, and God uses that honesty to deepen compassion, resilience, and trust.

Discussion Questions

  • Looking back, what is one heated season of your life that made you more compassionate or resilient?
  • How does sharing the heat with other people change what suffering feels like?

Prayer

Ask the Lord to meet you in places that feel too hot to endure alone and to make you the kind of person who can stay present with others in their pain.

Personal Practice

Reach out to someone you know is in the fire right now. Do not offer advice first. Offer your presence.

Additional Reading

Brew complete

You did not look away from suffering today. Compassionate presence — yours and God's — is the antidote to anxious isolation.
